KEY FACTS

  • Location

    Borders two prominent protected areas, the Yasuni National Park and the Cuyabeno Nature Reserve.

  • Population

    302 indigenous Kichwa families, 1,652 peoples.

  • Threats

    Oil prospecting has been the single greatest threat facing this community and continues to be critical, especially in the face of the much conflicted oil drilling that is set to take place in 2016 by the national oil company PetroAmazonas in Yasuni's Ishpingo-Tambococha-Tiputini (ITT) block, which covers vast tracts of pristine rainforest.

  • Project Plan

    The artisans project in Sani Isla uses traditional beadwork, to create sustainable incomes and empower women to provide for their families through entrepreneurial opportunities.

  • Curious Facts

    This area is among one of the most biologically diverse areas in the world. In fact, a single hectare in this region contains more species than all the wildlife in North America.

In the Ecuadorian Amazon, Rainforest Partnership is working with the indigenous community of Sani Isla. Now in its sixth year, this project showcases the remarkable story of the women in a remote rainforest community and a partnership that has empowered them to be economically sustainable. The women’s success in developing their Sani Warmi business producing exquisite handmade accessories from seeds and natural fibers, has strengthened the women’s resolve in standing up to outside interests and become a strong voice for rainforest conservation.


One of the biggest motivations for the women remains their desire and ability to send their children to school; and, one of their stated goals from 2015 and beyond is to fund the higher education of one girl every year from their common funds.

"One cannot spend time in the rainforest with the Achuar people without being deeply affected." - Dr. JoAnne Mowczko, The Achuar of the Pastaza River

Project Objectives

  • Construct a common community well for clean water

  • Help find markets for the Achuar people to sell their medicine & crafts
  • Provide Cultural and Environmental Education
